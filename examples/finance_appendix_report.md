# Q3 Finance Review

## Executive Summary

Revenue improved modestly in Q3, but leadership spent most of the quarter reducing refinancing risk and protecting covenant headroom. The detailed borrowing schedule lives in Appendix G, while the covenant amendment notes live in Appendix H.

## Operating Performance

Gross margin improved by 120 basis points after the company exited two low-yield service contracts. Operating expenses were flat quarter over quarter, and management reiterated its full-year efficiency target.

## Liquidity Overview

Cash from operations was directed toward reducing the revolving facility and smoothing near-term maturities. Management described leverage as improving, even though the formal debt schedule sits outside the core narrative sections.

## Appendix G - Debt Schedule

At September 30, short-term borrowings were $61 million versus $84 million at June 30. Long-term notes stayed at $210 million, and revolving credit availability improved to $145 million after scheduled repayments.

## Appendix H - Covenant Notes

The amended lending agreement reduced the minimum interest coverage threshold from 3.0x to 2.5x through the next two quarters. Management said the amendment was negotiated to preserve flexibility while debt balances stepped down.
